Species Spotlight: Northern Pine Snake

Pituophis melanoleucus melanoleucus

Introduction

The Northern Pine Snake is a bold, burrowing colubrid native to the eastern United States. Known for its striking pattern, deep hissing, and confident demeanour, it’s a favourite among keepers who love a bit of drama in their snake room.

Subspecies & Range

The Pine Snake is part of a broader species complex, including:

  • Northern Pine Snake – Pituophis melanoleucus melanoleucus – Eastern U.S.
  • Florida Pine Snake – P. m. mugitus – Florida & southern Georgia
  • Black Pine Snake – P. m. lodingi – Alabama & Mississippi (protected)

Appearance

These snakes can reach 5–7 feet in length. Northern subspecies often show bold black saddles on a pale ground colour, with a powerful head and keeled scales that give a rugged texture.

Behaviour

Not shy! Pine snakes are active, intelligent, and highly inquisitive. When threatened, they may flatten their head, vibrate their tail, and emit a loud, guttural hiss through their glottis. But in captivity, with regular handling, most settle into calm and curious pets.

Captive Care

  • Enclosure: 4–6ft minimum. Provide depth for burrowing and climbing enrichment.
  • Substrate: Aspen, soil mix, or cypress mulch – needs to support digging.
  • Temperature: Warm end around 30°C (86°F), cool end around 22°C (72°F).
  • Feeding: Typically large rodents. Great feeders, but avoid obesity.
  • Temperament: Often vocal, rarely aggressive. Very intelligent.

Why We Love Them

Pine snakes are underrated. They’re hardy, engaging, and full of personality. Their confidence makes them a great display animal – and their look is like no other. If you want a colubrid with a bit of flair, this is it.
