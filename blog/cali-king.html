California Kingsnake Care Guide

Published 10 July 2025

📊 Smart Facts: California Kingsnake

  • Scientific name: Lampropeltis californiae
  • Adult size: 3 to 4.5 feet
  • Lifespan: 15–20 years
  • Vivarium size: 4x2x2 ft minimum (5 ft ideal)
  • Temperature: 28–30°C (warm side), 22°C (cool side)
  • Humidity: 40–60%
  • Temperament: Alert, curious, occasionally food-aggressive
  • Beginner-friendly: ✅ Yes, with calm handling. Strong food response

California Kingsnakes are hardy, curious, and beautifully patterned colubrids with a reputation for being both easy to keep and entertaining to observe. Native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, they thrive in arid to semi-humid environments and have become one of the most popular pet snakes in the world.

🏠 Enclosure Requirements

These snakes are active and enjoy exploring, so give them room to roam. A 3x2x2 ft enclosure is the bare minimum for an adult, though a 4 ft length allows for better enrichment and temperature gradients. Glass or PVC enclosures both work well, as long as the space is secure and well ventilated.

Use substrate such as aspen, cypress mulch, or paper. Provide multiple hides, a medium-sized water dish, and safe climbing decor like branches and cork bark.

🌡️ Heating & Lighting

  • Basking spot: 28–30°C (82–86°F)
  • Cool end: 22°C (72°F)
  • Night drop: 18–20°C if your room cools naturally

No UVB is strictly required, but a low-level 2–5% UVB light can be beneficial for natural rhythms and long-term health. Always use thermostats and digital thermometers.

🦴 Feeding & Digestion

  • Hatchlings: Every 5 days (pinkies)
  • Juveniles: Every 7 days (fuzzies/small mice)
  • Adults: Every 10–14 days (adult mice)

California Kingsnakes are excellent feeders, but can become overly food-focused. Always feed with tongs and avoid handling for 48h post-feed. Track feeding dates to spot patterns — for help, check out our data tracking post.

✋ Handling

Most kings are calm with regular, confident handling. However, they are strong for their size and may be jumpy at first. Handle 2–3x per week in short sessions, always supporting their body and allowing them to explore at their own pace.

Fun fact: Kingsnakes get their name from their ability to eat other snakes — even venomous ones. So avoid housing them with anything else, including other kings.

💡 Enrichment

These snakes are inquisitive and benefit from environmental changes. Rearranging décor, offering burrowing zones, and rotating hides can all encourage natural behaviours. Many keepers report kings exploring shortly after changes.

⚠️ Watch Outs

  • Food aggression: Always open the enclosure with a hook tap or tool
  • Escape artists: Secure all lids — kings are notorious for testing boundaries
  • Overhandling: Respect their space if they hiss, musk, or try to escape
  • Shedding issues: Offer a moist hide or lightly mist during shed cycles

✅ Summary

California Kingsnakes are an ideal choice for both beginner and intermediate reptile keepers. With manageable size, strong feeding habits, and captivating patterns, they’re one of the most rewarding colubrids to care for. Just give them space, consistency, and enrichment — and they’ll thrive for decades.
